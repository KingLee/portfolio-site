A microsite that explores collaboration throughout history.

A project while working with O3 World.

One of the big marketing pushes for Autodesk 360 is the idea of seamless collaboration through multiple devices, people and projects. Taking this idea of collaboration, the team over at SEER Interactive came up with the concept of creating this walking timeline highlighting the ways in which people collaborated from the past until the present day.

The 50's were definitely all black and white.

There are a few themes going on here throughout the decades. The first, and most obvious is the background buildings change out to accompany the facts of that decade. The second one is in the color palette. It starts in the 50's with a black and white palette and increasingly gets more colors added and an increase in saturation.

Breaking out the illustration skills

Not only were the buildings drawn by hand in illustrator, so were the scenes within the highlight circle. There was an iconic background building and a highlight scene (the circle) for each decade and the generic buildings that needed to be created initially and adjusted for each scene. One small deviation was the 80's where I got to draw a mid-80s sports car. Bonus points if you can name the make and model.

It was quite the load of work! The pen tool has never felt so heavy. In the end though, it was well worth all the effort.

Watching it all come together.

This project was fun for a few reasons. One, I got to make a walk animation cycle for the main character. Secondly, I got to illustrate some pretty iconic buildings, and create a few generic ones. Overall, with it starting from an idea and a spreadsheet, one of the most fun problems was tackling the consistency and functional systems in the transitions from decade to decade.

I had a lot of conversations and check-ins with my front end developer, but in the end, we put together a great site that fit the project specifications. If we only had more time to vary the generic building illustrations and add a mobile friendly version!
