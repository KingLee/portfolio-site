The Griffin

NISL.net was in need of a new take on their web presence.

A project while working with O3 World.

NISL came to O3 World to update their web identity. After an extensive wireframing process, the project was handed off to me for visual design. I took a flat, modern approach to the UI to try and present the information as cleanly as possible. Additionally, using people and faces wherever I could, helped with cementing a feeling of trust throughout the site.

Targeting the decision makers.

Part of the goal here is to be able to offer enough information to principles or administrators, backed by facts, so they can request NISL's services for their district or school. We created sections targeting these decision makers specifically.

Custom icons! The fun parts.

Anytime I get to make custom icons, a small part of me glows on the inside (hopefully it's not radioactive). NISL had a need for custom icons to call out certain characterstics found in leaders. Here are the forms for those icons and they can be found in context on the full site.

Custom Google Maps Skin.

Taking the details a step further, we decided to create a custom google map with NISL's colors. The map serves as a visual centerpiece to show the support and broad reach of NISL's programs.
