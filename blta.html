A responsive portfolio site for BLTa.

A project while working with O3 World.

BLTa's needs were rather simple. They had a site built in flash and needed to enter the modern world with a site that would not only work, but also be optimized for smartphones and tablets. Taking desktop wireframes and their established brand, I was able to evolve their site into a modern, responsive website.

The decision to go flat.

Following on the heals of the NISL.net project, BLTa got a similar flat UI treatment, but for slightly different reasons. Taking a look at the wireframes, there was a lot of accessory information and clickable elements per page. In addition to this, architecture photography is busy with repeating patterns and a lot of elements in the photo itself. A flatter UI was chosen for it's ability to be less overwhelming to the user.

Systems around the "L"

One of the biggest branding elements that we had to keep was the incorporation of the "L". You can see it in these images where it is holding accessory information to the left and bottom of the content area. Different kinds of content had to fit this area, really limiting our design options. But what's a good design problem without a few strict rules? In the end I think we came up with creative ways to use the space in a variety of ways.
