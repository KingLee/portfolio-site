My Favorite Photoshop Helpers

Photoshop: The Swiss Army Knife of Web Design

Who doesn't want to work faster? In the agency world, or really any world for that matter, productivity and efficiency are as critical as creativity when it comes to the final product. Because everyone’s time is valuable, I’m giving you a list of the most useful extensions that exist for working with Photoshop.

GuideGuide

This tool is invaluable for setting up grids with correct math. Before GuideGuide, a designer would do a bunch of math in their head to figure out their column widths, margins and gutters, etc. Then, come the errors. A forgotten margin here, a miscalculated column there. This continues to be the source of many a headache.

Luckily, there doesn’t have to be guesswork anymore!

After you become familiar with GuideGuide, it is a great tool to get your grid up and running in under a minute. When you make a wrong assumption in your math, you will know it is off before you get too far. Or you can just let GuideGuide do the math for you. It’s awesome like that.

An extra handy feature of GuideGuide is the ability to save preset grids. Do you love working in a 4 column grid with 40px margins? Great, save it! Now it is only a single click away.

Renamy

Don’t you hate it when you copy a layer and that pesky “copy” tag gets dropped on every new layer? There is a fix for that. Don’t you also hate when you make ~17 shapes and forget to name them appropriately? Enter Renamy. Easily rename multiple layers at a time with this useful extension.

This extension is really great for quickly cleaning up layers.

Flaticon

Are you working in all vector yet? You should be. What about those social icons that make their way into every comp you have ever worked on? Do you keep illustrator open and drag them in for every comp? I know that’s what I used to do.

Check out Flaticon! Easily keep a repository of commonly used vector shapes within Photoshop with this extension. It is great for keeping social icons a few clicks away, or other icons that get commonly used in designs.

LiveShare PS

LiveShare PS is brand new! In Photoshop CC 2014, the guys over at InVision app worked with Adobe to create this crazy ass extension. With this, you can start a live video screen share with other designers to have impromptu design meetings. Along with showing your screen and having people be able to see updates in real time, you can present your work in a variety of ways including mirroring your designs to a physical mobile device.

It is free, definitely grab this one!

WebZap

Norm, the guy who runs UIParade is a man on a mission. He has a bunch of really great photoshop extensions to help out web designers and developers. WebZap has some neat functionality to help designers create some basic layout functionality and really great typography shortcuts.

SubtlePatterns

If you don’t know about SubtlePatterns yet, you are going to be delighted with this one. Who hasn’t spent countless hours on this site, looking through textures to use on a variety of web assets? With this extension, you get the whole library in Photoshop at the tips of your fingers.

Much better than downloading everything and manually uploading them to your textures palette!

Doco

Doco is one of my newer finds as well. It is a great new way to visualize your open tabs in photoshop and transfer assets in a quick and efficient way. Easily close tabs, copy assets to multiple documents, or merge documents with this extension. Sometimes the smaller tools have the biggest impact.

SpecKing

SpecKing is a really good knitty gritty extension used for design documentation, or for front end devs that need to measure UI elements / padding / margins. “Red-lining” as the suits call it, draws a line between elements and tags a little label with the measurement. This extension does that for you automatically.

SpecKing also has a nice inspection on font elements. Easily grab font information from a layer to translate it into your CSS document.

That Concludes My Helpfulness for Today
